Chess Basics

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on an 8x8 grid. Each player starts with 16 pieces, including a king, queen, bishops, knights, rooks, and pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king.

Chess Rules

Chess rules govern the movement of each piece. Pawns move forward, bishops diagonally, knights in an 'L' shape, rooks horizontally or vertically, queens in any direction, and kings one square in any direction.

Chess Titles and Norms

FIDE awards titles such as Grandmaster (GM) and International Master (IM) to top players. Players achieve norms by meeting performance criteria in tournaments, contributing to title aspirations.
